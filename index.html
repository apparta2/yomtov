Yom Tov – Artist's Statement
For the last 10-15 years, my work focused has on interpretation of Torah themes. During the years, I devoted many of my oil paintings to The Days of Creation. This enormous theme brought me to the border of the non-material, invisible structures.
Rupture and Repair in Hebrew is "Shvira veTikkun". These are stages of the whole process of Creation. We know from Ariza"l's book Etz Chaim – The Tree of Life, that HaShem created this world after worlds were destroyed. The conflict and harmony between positive and destructive powers, blessing and curse, unity and separation are part of the non-stop process of Creation. In the spiritual reality, the Rupture is never final and the Repair is never complete. We know from our Sages that in the near future we have a Final Redemption and Gmar HaTikkun which is part of HaShem's plan to bring this world completion. I attempt to address these issues in some of my work.
I am submitting my series Conception of Light which includes oil paintings from the last 3 years: Conception of Light, Alter in the Temple, Hidden Manifestation, Blue Invasion, Jerusalem Domes and Jerusalem Rhythm which address my interpretation of bringing harmony from separate and contrasting parts of colors and space through transformation.
Yom Tov
Jerusalem, November 2008

Eli Yomtov

A lifetime artist dedicated to our Torah 

CALL

Yom Tov is a free-style artist. His passion and his art - oil paintings and drawings -- are devoted to biblical themes with expressionistic and romantic interpretations. Yom Tov believes that visual commentaries based on original Jewish holy texts and Jerusalem landscapes bring to public-at-large beauty and harmony of Jewish culture. The last few years he has been working on a continuing series of Days of Creation, Kings and Prophets, Kohelet, and Hidden Light. 


For the last 15 years Yom Tov has held many gallery and private shows around the world.

His paintings and hand-graphics were shown in museums and private collections in Israel, US, France, Switzerland, Japan, Norway, Belgium and Russia. His work has been in many Jewish cultural centers around the US, in places such as: Boca Raton, New York, White Plains, Los Angeles, Palo Alto, San Francisco, Kansas City, Boston, Denver, Dallas and others.

In 2008, Yom Tov partipated in the first international ArtJerusalem fair. In 2011 he did a solo show, “Kohelet” at the Heichal Shlomo Jewish Art Museum. Presently he is exhibiting “Kings and Prophets” at the Wohl Archeological Museum in the Jewish Quarter, Jerusalem. In addition, his work has been shown in:

 National Jewish Museum, Washington , DC  
 Chan Museum, Askelon , Israel
 Cinema Museum, Moscow
 Lubavitch Rebbe Museum, Moscow
 Literature Museum, Moscow
 Galerie de la Corraterie, Geneva
 Siano Gallery, Philadelphia , PA
 Branchville Soho Gallery, Ridgefield , CT
 Eden Gallery, Jerusalem
 Edelweisscard Headquarters, Geneva
 Art Exhibit, Rodeo Drive, Beverly Hills, California
 The World Market Center, Las Vegas

YomTov started his art career studying under Roman Vainshtock (1932-1988), one of the unique artists and art teachers in the South Ukraine. He studied at the Moscow Architecture Institute, majoring in architecture and urban planning, theory and history from 1980-1984. He received is Ph.D. in 1987 under Prof. Oleg Shwidkowski (1925-1993). He was staff scholar at the Research Institute of Arts Theory and History at the Russian Academy of Arts, Moscow in 1987-1994, in addition to painting and participating in art shows.
